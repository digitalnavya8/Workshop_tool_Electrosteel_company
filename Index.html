The Career ROI Framework

Fill each box for your child. Don't overthink — write the first honest thing that comes to mind. There are no wrong answers.

Interests
What does your child love doing?
Natural Strengths
What comes easily without effort?
Market Demand 2030
What will the world pay well for?
ROI Sweet Spot
Where all three overlap

💡 Navya's Insight: Most parents focus only on what the market wants. But if there's no interest and no strength in it, your child will be average at best — and miserable at worst. The overlap is where your child becomes exceptional.

Common Family Mistakes — Check Your Blind Spots

Tap each mistake you recognise from your own experience. Awareness is the first step.

  • Choosing stream based on neighbour/cousin's success, not child's own profile
  • Confusing high marks in a subject with genuine aptitude or interest in it
  • Assuming Science → Engineering → Job Security (this is a 1990s map)
  • Not discussing career options with the child — deciding for them without them
  • Waiting until Class 12 results to think about career direction
  • Underestimating Arts and Commerce as valid high-ROI career paths
  • Ignoring emerging careers — only considering professions that already exist today
